Biryani Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 4-5 green cardamoms
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp red chili powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Saffron threads soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk
  • 4 cups water

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Wash and soak the basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
    • In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add salt to taste.
    • Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is 70% cooked (parboiled). Drain and set aside.
  2. Marinate the Chicken:
    • In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
    • Mix well and let it marinate for at least 1 hour (preferably overnight for better flavor).
  3. Cook the Chicken:
    • Heat the oil and ghee in a large pan over medium heat.
    • Add cumin seeds, cloves, green cardamoms, bay leaves, and cinnamon stick. Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
    • Add the sliced onions and cook until golden brown.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and oil starts to separate.
    • Add the marinated chicken to the pan. Cook until the chicken is well-cooked and the oil separates from the masala.
    • Sprinkle garam masala, chopped coriander, and mint leaves. Mix well.
  4. Layer the Biryani:
    • In a heavy-bottomed pot, start layering the biryani. Begin with a layer of the cooked chicken at the bottom.
    • Follow with a layer of parboiled rice.
    • Sprinkle some saffron milk over the rice.
    • Repeat the layers until all the chicken and rice are used up, finishing with a layer of rice on top.
    • Drizzle the remaining saffron milk over the top layer.
  5. Dum Cooking:
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal the edges with dough to ensure no steam escapes.
    • Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the rice to fully cook.
    • Turn off the heat and let it rest for another 10 minutes before opening the lid.
  6. Serve:
    • Gently fluff the rice with a fork, being careful not to break the grains.
    • Serve hot, garnished with more fresh coriander and mint leaves.
    • Enjoy your delicious homemade biryani!

Extra Tips:

  • Rice Quality: Use high-quality basmati rice for the best texture and flavor.
  • Marination Time: Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour, but overnight is preferable for deeper flavor.
  • Layering: Ensure even layering for consistent flavor distribution. Start with the meat and end with the rice.
  • Cooking on Dum: Seal the pot tightly to prevent steam from escaping, ensuring the rice cooks evenly and absorbs all the flavors.
  • Saffron: Soak saffron threads in warm milk to extract maximum color and flavor.
  • Onion Frying: Fry onions until golden brown for a rich flavor and crispy texture.
  • Fresh Herbs: Use fresh coriander and mint leaves for an aromatic and vibrant biryani.
  • Resting Time: Allow the biryani to rest for a few minutes after cooking to let the flavors settle.
  • Serving: Serve with raita or a simple salad to complement the rich flavors of the biryani.
