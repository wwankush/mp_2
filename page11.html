Fish Fry Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 4 fish fillets (such as tilapia, cod, or catfish)
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornmeal
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the fish fillets under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels.
  2. Place the fish fillets in a large bowl and pour the buttermilk over them. Let them soak for 15-20 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix together the flour, cornmeal, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Heat about 1 inch of vegetable oil in a large skillet or frying pan over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C).
  5. Remove the fish fillets from the buttermilk, allowing the excess to drip off. Dredge each fillet in the flour mixture, ensuring an even coat. Shake off any excess flour.
  6. Carefully add the coated fish fillets to the hot oil. Fry in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan.
  7. Fry the fish for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy. The internal temperature should reach 145°F (63°C).
  8. Remove the fish from the oil and place it on a wire rack or paper towels to drain excess oil.
  9. Serve the fish hot with lemon wedges on the side. Enjoy your delicious fried fish!

Extra Tips:

  • For extra crispy fish, double-dip the fillets: after the first coating of the flour mixture, dip them back in the buttermilk and then coat them again with the flour mixture.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, increase the amount of cayenne pepper in the flour mixture or add a dash of hot sauce to the buttermilk soak.
  • Maintain the oil temperature by using a thermometer. If the oil is too hot, the fish will burn before cooking through; if it's too cool, the fish will be greasy.
  • Serve with tartar sauce, coleslaw, or fries for a complete meal.
  • Leftover fried fish can be reheated in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10-15 minutes to maintain its crispiness.
